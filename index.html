Profile

Alex MacMillan is a barrister practising in English employment law. He has extensive experience across the full spectrum of employment litigation, including unfair dismissal, discrimination, whistleblowing, and contractual disputes. He regularly appears in Employment Tribunals, the Employment Appeal Tribunal, and the High Court.

He is known for his incisive analysis, robust advocacy, and commercial approach to litigation. He is instructed by a wide range of clients, from multinational corporations to senior executives.

Recent Developments

Fictional Case: Smith v. Acme Corp (2024)

The Employment Appeal Tribunal (EAT) has provided new guidance on the territorial scope of the Equality Act 2010 in the context of remote-working employees. The ruling clarifies that an employee working exclusively from a foreign jurisdiction, but for a UK-based employer and with their work having a 'strong connection' to Great Britain, may still fall under the Act's protection. The key determinant was the nature of the contractual obligations and the operational integration into the British business entity.
